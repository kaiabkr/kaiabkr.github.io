WHAT'S THE RIGHT APPROACH TO SOLVING THIS ISSUE?

In efforts to protect and better the quality of living for displaced minority communities, cities should
aim to desegregate these neighborhoods. They could approach this by stabilizing property taxes for those
living in gentrified and mixed-income communities. Cities should also allocate more and better quality
resources for low-income minority communities. While modernization is not an entirely negative initiative,
redevelopment projects need to have widespread benefits that allow current residents to remain in their neighborhoods.

Ultimately, racial wealth disparities and discrimination require attention in order to address gentrification.
